Project 1: Sentiment Analysis on Movie Reviews

This project is a Python-based web application that utilizes Natural Language Processing (NLP) and a pre-trained BERT (Bidirectional Encoder Representations from Transformers) model to analyze sentiment in movie reviews. Designed to be user-friendly, it offers an interactive web interface where users can submit movie reviews and quickly receive an analysis indicating whether the sentiment of the review is positive or negative. The application showcases the practical application of advanced machine learning techniques in processing and understanding human language, making it an exemplary project for anyone interested in AI, NLP, and data science. Through its development, I honed my skills in Python, Flask, working with NLP models, and deploying web applications, making it a significant addition to my portfolio as a demonstration of my technical capabilities and my passion for machine learning and natural language processing.

Project 2: Customer Segmentation

This project employs RFM (Recency, Frequency, Monetary Value) analysis and K-Means clustering on the 'Online Retail II' dataset for customer segmentation in a retail context. The aim is to segment customers based on their purchase behaviors, allowing for targeted marketing strategies. The dataset contains transactional data, which is cleaned and processed to calculate RFM metrics for each customer. These metrics are then used to segment customers into distinct groups using K-Means clustering, guided by the Elbow Method to determine the optimal number of clusters. The outcome is a set of customer segments that can be targeted with personalized communication, enhancing customer engagement, loyalty, and sales. This project illustrates the practical application of machine learning in marketing analytics to understand and leverage customer behavior.

Project 4: Music & Mental Health: The Rhythmic Impact on Well-being

Intrigued by the harmonious relationship between music genres and mental health, my team and I embarked on an analytical journey with a Kaggle dataset originating from a University of Washington study. With self-reported mental health conditions as our notes and Tableau as our instrument, we orchestrated an in-depth analysis that resonated with intriguing findings.

Our symphony of data revealed a melody of impacts: some music genres serenaded the mind with positive vibes, while others struck a dissonant chord. The data sang of high notes, particularly among younger and older demographics, where the therapeutic tones of certain genres amplified their mental well-being.

Yet, like any complex composition, variations arose. Self-reporting biases, cultural harmonics, and the scale of our sample size added layers to our understanding. Delving deeper, we tuned into specific genres, such as the dynamic world of video game music, uncovering patterns and potential for enhancing mood and alleviating conditions when the right tunes are played early in life.

Discover the power of music on mental health through our visualized tableau of insights. Explore how the right playlist could be a key to harmonizing mental wellness across ages.

Project 6: Personal Consumption Expenditures Prediction: Gasoline and other Energy goods

For our fourth project, "Exploration of Personal Consumption Expenditures in the USA - Nondurable Goods: Gasoline and Other Energy Goods," we turned financial detectives with a magnifying glass on the dance between oil prices and how much green Americans spill on energy goods. Cracking the code with Holt and ARIMA models, our data wizardry forecasted a whopping $481 billion splash in PCE by Q4 2023. It was a journey back to the future, from 1947 to 2023, proving that when oil prices sway, PCE for gasoline and energy goods grooves along. So, buckle up; our analysis signals a hefty fuel bill on the horizon, powered by the undying romance between oil prices and energy expenditures.
